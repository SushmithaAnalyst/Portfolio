Operations · Demand Optimization 03

The bikes were there. They just weren't where people needed them.

Availability isn't the problem : redistribution is. By analyzing demand at the station level, a clear picture emerged: weather barely matters, weekends barely shift demand, but 6 PM is where everything breaks down. Commuters flood stations that weren't stocked for them.

The fix: Station-level rebalancing timed to evening commuter peaks , not broad network averages , reduces shortages at high-demand nodes and idle inventory at low-demand ones at the same time.

The Red Line is coming. Roseland isn't ready.

Transit access without economic access doesn't move communities forward. Content Analytics · Tableau 05

Views don't mean success. 537 videos prove it.

Thirteen years of YouTube data. 537 videos. Every platform metric you can name. The single strongest predictor of long-term performance isn't subscriber count, production budget, or total views , it's what happens in the first 60 days. That's the window most content teams aren't watching closely enough.

The counterintuitive finding: High-view videos consistently underperformed on engagement rate. Content that drove early interaction compounded into better long-term outcomes : a fundamentally different optimization target than most teams use.

Aviation · Financial Analysis 06

Spirit Airlines : Ceased Operations Analysis

A comprehensive 34-year autopsy of Spirit Airlines' collapse , from ULCC pioneer to double bankruptcy and full shutdown on May 2, 2026. Traced $2.5B+ in cumulative losses, the DOJ-blocked JetBlue merger, and the cascade of failures that stranded 1.8M passengers.

Key finding: COVID-19 devastation, an $8.1B debt load, and a blocked $3.8B lifeline left no recovery path. Post-shutdown, fares on Spirit routes rose 23% as Frontier absorbed 100+ routes overnight.
